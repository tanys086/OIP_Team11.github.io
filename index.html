Measuring Kibble Palace Footfall with LiDAR Technology

Help the Kibble Palace accurately count visitor numbers using innovative LiDAR sensing technology that respects the building's heritage status while providing detailed analytics.

The Challenge

The Kibble Palace, a 19th-century glasshouse and Category A Listed building, attracts visitors worldwide but lacks accurate visitor counting systems. Glasgow City Council needs detailed footfall analytics without compromising the building's heritage integrity.

üì°

Our LiDAR Solution

A non-invasive LiDAR-based visitor counting system that provides real-time analytics without physical modifications to the building. Our solution respects Listed Building regulations while delivering precise visitor data and behavioral insights.

üìä

The Impact

Accurate visitor analytics broken down by time, date, weather conditions, and events. Interactive dashboards displaying visitor patterns and country of origin data to optimize operations and enhance visitor experience.

Technical Approach

LiDAR Sensor Network

Strategic placement of non-invasive LiDAR sensors at key entry and exit points to accurately detect and count visitors without requiring structural modifications to the heritage building.

Real-time Analytics

Live dashboard displaying current visitor counts, peak times, and visitor flow patterns with data segmentation by time of day, weather conditions, and special events.

Heritage Compliance

Solution designed specifically for Category A Listed buildings, ensuring no physical alterations while providing comprehensive visitor tracking suitable for historic plant and animal environments.

Data Visualization

Interactive dashboards showing visitor demographics, popular areas within the Palace, and origin country data to support marketing and operational decisions.

Project Timeline

Week 1: Research & Discovery

Conducted site analysis of the Kibble Palace, researched Listed Building constraints, investigated existing visitor counting technologies, and explored LiDAR capabilities for heritage environments.

Week 2: LiDAR Prototyping & Testing

Developed LiDAR sensor placement strategies, created data visualization prototypes, tested counting algorithms, and designed dashboard interfaces for Glasgow City Council stakeholders.
